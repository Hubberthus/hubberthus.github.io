About me

My name is Norbert Fekete.

I'm just a computer programmer who happens to like playing with microcontrollers. I'm in no way connected to Espressif and those creating modules for the ESP32 SoC.

I work as a developer at an international company, and this page is just one of my hobby projects.

I started to do all this for myself, but decided to share with the community. Maybe it can help out others as well.

It is completely free and open-source. Feel free to copy, but please put my name somewhere if you put your version somewhere.

Why?

Before I bought my first ESP32 module, I looked deep into the documentation, and a bit into the source of the ESP-IDF.

At first I was completely blown away by its rich contents. Then I realized that setting up a project for this chip will be a challenge. It has so many peripherals with overlapping pins, and then there is the GPIO matrix which is a powerful tool, and moves complicated to a whole new level.

For ESP8266 I've been using the Arduino core, but it was clear for me that the ESP32 will be a lot harder to do as an Arduino. It's not impossible, just not the best idea. Sure, there are many libraries for the Arduino, and those who prefer that I understand. But if we want its full potential, then the ESP-IDF is the good choice.

And after choosing the ESP-IDF over Arduino I was scratching my head, how to choose the right pinout settings for my projects? What if I would like to change somethig a bit and that needed changing the whole setup? And I need to be aware of the input-only pins.

This is when I saw my brother use the STM32Cube, and that was the spark for this all. Love at fist sight, that what what I needed. I won't deny it, this is a kind-of copy with some modifications. And made as a website, rather than a program.

Why a website?

It is kind of cross-platform and you don't need to download anything. Just have a recent browser, select your module, and start playing with pin settings.
